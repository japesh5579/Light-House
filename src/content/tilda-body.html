We are devoted to the traditional path of Hatha Yoga, embracing its essence and ancient wisdom. Occasionally, in harmony with the flow of the class, we gently introduce elements from the primary series of Ashtanga Yoga, allowing you to explore different dimensions of your practice.

As you delve into the world of asanas, you will not only master the postures but also learn to create your own sequences and share the gift of yoga with others. To apply for an Indian eVisa, travelers need to complete an online application form, providing personal information, travel details, and uploading supporting documents, such as a passport-sized photograph and a scanned copy of the passport's bio page. The application is then processed by the Indian authorities, who assess the information and make a decision regarding the visa approval.

Upon approval, the eVisa is electronically linked to the traveler's passport and sent via email. It is essential to carry a printed copy of the eVisa during the journey, as it will be verified by immigration officers upon arrival in India.

  • A recent applicant's photograph, passport-style
  • Credit or debit card to pay the India online visa fee

FAQs

Answers to Popular Questions

What kind of yoga do you teach at Lighthouse Krishna?

We are devoted to the traditional path of Hatha Yoga and Kriya Meditation, taught with authenticity on the banks of the Ganga in Rishikesh under the Krishna Ganga Foundation.

Is the 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training certified?

Yes — on completion and passing the exam, you receive dual accreditation from Yoga Alliance International and the World Yoga Federation, recognised worldwide.

What is Pankh and how is it connected to Lighthouse Krishna?

Pankh is our founders' sister initiative — an NGO founded by Alankrita Banerjee in 2013 that provides free education, vocational training and healthcare to underprivileged children and women across India.

How can I support or donate to Pankh?

You can donate directly through Pankh to fund education, vocational training and essential care for children and women in need — every contribution creates lasting change.

Who can join the Lighthouse Krishna training programs?

Seekers from across the globe are welcome — whether you're new to yoga or an experienced practitioner ready to deepen your practice through our 200-Hour YTT, MTC or Alankrita Banerjee's courses.

Where are Lighthouse Krishna and Pankh located?

Both are based in Rishikesh, Uttarakhand, India — Lighthouse Krishna on the banks of the Ganges, with Pankh's outreach extending to communities across India.

Please note that installment plans extending beyond the Early Bird deadline will be charged at the standard course price.

Payment Deadlines

Credit Transfer / “Support a Friend” Policy
At Lighthouse Krishna, we understand that unexpected life circumstances can arise, and we aim to offer flexibility and compassion wherever possible.
If a student decides they are no longer able to attend their training and still has an eligible remaining course credit with Lighthouse Krishna (whether partial credit, 50% retained credit, or full transferable credit), we offer the option to transfer this credit to another student.
This may be used as:
  • A gifted training contribution to a friend or family member
  • A “Support a Friend” offering to help another student join their Yoga Teacher Training
  • A form of Karma Yoga, allowing students to pass forward the opportunity for growth and learning

Conditions:
  • Credit transfers must be approved by the Lighthouse Krishna team.
  • Credits are non-refundable once transferred to another student.
  • The transferred student must use the credit within the original 18-month validity period.
  • Credits cannot be exchanged for cash.
  • Any remaining balance difference must be paid by the new student if applicable.
